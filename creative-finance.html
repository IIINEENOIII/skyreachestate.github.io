Creative finance — what it is, and when it helps.

Sometimes a traditional “cash offer” isn’t the best solution. In certain situations, alternative structures can create a cleaner outcome for a seller — especially when timeline or equity is tight.

Important: Not every property or seller qualifies. We’ll explain the risks and only propose options that make sense.

Seller Financing

You (the seller) act like the bank. Buyer pays you over time.

  • Can help if you want monthly income instead of a lump sum.
  • Often used when a property needs work or financing is tricky.
  • Terms vary (down payment, interest rate, length).

Subject-To (Existing Mortgage)

Buyer purchases the home while the existing mortgage stays in place (title transfers).

  • Can help when payments are behind and speed matters.
  • Requires careful documentation and expectations.
  • There are risks; we’ll explain them clearly before anything happens.

Wrap / Wraparound Structure

A new agreement “wraps” around an existing loan, with one payment stream.

  • Can be used when interest rates or terms make a creative structure attractive.
  • Must be structured carefully and legally.
  • Not available in every scenario.

Lease Option

Tenant-buyer leases now with an option to buy later.

  • Sometimes used when a seller wants time or a higher price later.
  • Not ideal for every distressed scenario.
  • Requires clear written terms and strong screening.

Novation-Style Sale (Explained Simply)

A structured sale that can help maximize price while handling repairs/marketing logistics.

  • Can be useful when you want higher sale price but can’t manage renovations.
  • Not instant—timeline depends on the market.
  • We’ll only propose this when it fits your situation.

When cash is best

Fast, simple, clean.

  • Foreclosure deadlines
  • Vacant / vandalism risk
  • Major repairs
  • Estate sale needing closure

What sellers usually care about

  • Speed: how fast can it close?
  • Certainty: will it fall apart?
  • Privacy: do I need showings?
  • Net proceeds: what do I walk away with?
We’ll walk you through these in plain English, no hype.
